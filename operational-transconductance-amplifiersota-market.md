<p><p>Operational Transconductance Amplifiers (OTA) find applications in various markets such as multiplexers, voltage followers, current-controlled amplifiers, filters, multipliers, comparators, and others. In a multiplexer, OTAs are used to select one input from multiple sources. OTAs act as voltage followers to isolate input and output stages. They can function as current-controlled amplifiers to regulate the output current. OTAs are also used in filters for signal processing. In multipliers, they provide the product of two input signals. Additionally, OTAs are employed in comparators to compare input voltages.</p></p>
