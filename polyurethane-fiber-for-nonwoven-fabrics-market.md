<p><p>Polyurethane fiber for nonwoven fabrics market types are categorized based on their melt flow rate (MFR). MFR values determine the viscosity of the polymer, affecting the fiber properties. Fibers with MFR < 25 g/10 min are considered high viscosity, resulting in more rigid and durable fabrics. Those with MFR 25-40 g/10 min are medium viscosity fibers, offering a balance between strength and flexibility. Fibers with MFR > 40 g/10 min have low viscosity, making them more soft and pliable, suitable for applications requiring comfort and flexibility.</p></p>
