<p><p>Nonfat milk powder market types are categorized based on the processing treatment used to produce the product. Low-heat processing treatment involves drying milk at temperatures below 200°F, which helps retain the most nutrients and flavor. Medium-heat processing treatment involves drying milk at temperatures between 200-300°F, resulting in a slightly caramelized flavor. High-heat processing treatment involves drying milk at temperatures above 300°F, which results in a more robust flavor but may reduce some nutrients. Consumers can choose based on their preference for flavor and nutritional content.</p></p>
