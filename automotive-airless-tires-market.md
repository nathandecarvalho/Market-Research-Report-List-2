<p><p>Bias tires are constructed with multiple layers of rubber-coated fabric crisscrossed at angles, providing a sturdy and robust structure. These tires are known for their high load-carrying capacity and are often used in heavy-duty vehicles such as trucks and buses. On the other hand, radial tires are constructed with layers of rubber-coated steel belts running perpendicular to the direction of travel, offering better traction and fuel efficiency. They are commonly used in passenger cars and light commercial vehicles. Both types play a crucial role in the automotive airless tires market, catering to different vehicle types and preferences.</p></p>
