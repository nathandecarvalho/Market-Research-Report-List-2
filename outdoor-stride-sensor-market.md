<p><p>Outdoor Stride Sensors can be categorized into two main types based on how they measure movement: Gravity Induced and Vibration Induced. Gravity Induced sensors work by detecting changes in the orientation of the sensor as it moves with the user, while Vibration Induced sensors rely on vibrations caused by each step to measure stride length. Both types of sensors are popular in the market for outdoor activities such as running, cycling, and hiking, providing accurate data to help users track their performance and progress.</p></p>
