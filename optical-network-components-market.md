<p><ul><li>Synchronous Optical Networking</li><li>Fiber Channel</li><li>Wavelength Division Multiplexing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Synchronous Optical Networking (SONET) is a standardized multiplexing technique for transmitting multiple digital signals over fiber optic networks. Fiber Channel is a high-speed networking technology commonly used in storage area networks to connect computer systems and storage devices. Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) is a method of combining multiple optical signals onto a single fiber by using different wavelengths of light. These components are crucial for the efficient and reliable operation of optical networks, facilitating high-speed data transmission and network scalability.</p></p>
