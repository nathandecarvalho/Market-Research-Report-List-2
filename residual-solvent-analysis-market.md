<p><p>Residual Solvent Analysis refers to the process of analyzing and quantifying solvent residues left behind in pharmaceuticals, food, and other products. This analysis can be done using Gas Chromatography (GC) or Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC separates and detects individual components of a sample based on their molecular characteristics, while GC-MS combines GC with mass spectrometry to provide additional information on the molecular structure of the compounds. These techniques are essential in ensuring product safety and quality control.</p></p>
