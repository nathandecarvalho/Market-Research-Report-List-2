<p><p>Infant nasal CPAP therapy can be delivered through different market types, including High Flow Nasal Cannula (HFNC) and Mechanical CPAP. HFNC delivers a high flow of warm, humidified air through nasal prongs to support respiratory function, while Mechanical CPAP delivers a continuous positive pressure to keep the airways open and improve oxygenation. Both market types are commonly used in neonatal intensive care units to treat respiratory distress in infants, providing non-invasive respiratory support and reducing the need for intubation.</p></p>
