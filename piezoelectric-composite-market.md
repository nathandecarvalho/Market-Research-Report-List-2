<p><p>Piezoelectric composites are materials that exhibit piezoelectric properties, meaning they can generate electric charges when subjected to mechanical stress. There are two main types of piezoelectric composites: piezoelectric ceramics and piezoelectric polymers. Piezoelectric ceramics are inorganic materials with high piezoelectric coefficients and are commonly used in sensors, actuators, and transducers. Piezoelectric polymers, on the other hand, are organic materials that offer flexibility and are used in applications like wearable technologies, medical devices, and energy harvesting systems. Both types of piezoelectric composites have unique properties and applications in various industries.</p></p>
