<p><p>Dental X-ray sensors are used for capturing images of the teeth and surrounding structures. There are two main types of dental X-ray sensors: intraoral and extraoral. Intraoral X-rays are taken inside the mouth and provide detailed images of individual teeth and their roots. Extraoral X-rays are taken outside the mouth and provide a broader view of the entire oral cavity, including the jaw and skull. Both types of X-rays are essential for diagnosing dental issues and creating treatment plans.</p></p>
