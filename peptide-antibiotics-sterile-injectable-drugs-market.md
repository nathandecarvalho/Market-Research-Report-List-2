<p><p>Peptide antibiotics are a type of sterile injectable drug used for treating bacterial infections. There are two main types of peptide antibiotics: ribosomal synthesized peptides and non-ribosomal synthesized peptides. Ribosomal synthesized peptides are produced by ribosomes within the cell and include antibiotics like polymyxins. Non-ribosomal synthesized peptides, on the other hand, are created by non-ribosomal peptide synthetases and include antibiotics such as vancomycin. Both types of peptide antibiotics are important in the healthcare industry for combating bacterial infections.</p></p>
