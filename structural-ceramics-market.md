<p><p>Structural ceramics can be classified into two main types: Oxide Ceramics and Non-Oxide Ceramics. Oxide Ceramics include materials such as alumina, zirconia, and silica, which have high thermal and chemical stability. On the other hand, Non-Oxide Ceramics consist of materials like carbides, nitrides, and borides, which exhibit exceptional mechanical properties and wear resistance. Both types of structural ceramics find applications in industries such as aerospace, automotive, electronics, and healthcare due to their superior properties and performance.</p></p>
