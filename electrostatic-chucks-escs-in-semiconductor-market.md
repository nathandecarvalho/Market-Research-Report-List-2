<p><p>Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) in the semiconductor market are used to hold wafers during the manufacturing process using electrostatic force. There are two main types: Coulomb Type ESCs, which use direct contact between the chuck and the wafer, and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type ESCs, which use an insulating layer to create a gap between the chuck and the wafer. Both types offer unique advantages such as improved wafer flatness, reduced particle contamination, and enhanced thermal uniformity, catering to the diverse needs of the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
