<p><p>Liquid biofuels are alternative fuels derived from organic materials such as corn, sugar cane, vegetable oils, and animal fats. Three main types of liquid biofuels include ethanol, biodiesel, and hydrogenated vegetable oil (HVO). Ethanol is primarily made from fermenting sugars and starches from crops, while biodiesel is produced from animal fats and vegetable oils. HVO, on the other hand, is a renewable diesel fuel made by processing vegetable oils and fats through hydrogenation. These biofuels are considered cleaner and more sustainable alternatives to traditional fossil fuels.</p></p>
