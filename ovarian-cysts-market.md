<p><p>Ovarian cysts can be classified into two main types: functional cysts and non-functional cysts. Functional cysts are formed during the menstrual cycle and usually resolve on their own without the need for treatment. On the other hand, non-functional cysts are more serious and may require medical intervention, as they can be caused by underlying health conditions such as endometriosis or polycystic ovary syndrome. Understanding the differences between these two types of ovarian cysts is important in determining the appropriate treatment approach.</p></p>
