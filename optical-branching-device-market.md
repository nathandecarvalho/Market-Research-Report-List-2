<p><p>Optical branching devices are crucial components in fiber optic networks, enabling the splitting and distribution of optical signals to multiple paths. There are two main types in the market: Fiber Bragg Grating-based Couplers (FBC) and Planar Lightwave Circuit (PLC) splitters. FBC devices use fiber grating technology to split the signal, while PLC splitters utilize waveguide technology for signal distribution. Both technologies offer efficient and reliable ways to divide optical signals in various applications such as telecommunications, data centers, and cable TV networks.</p></p>
