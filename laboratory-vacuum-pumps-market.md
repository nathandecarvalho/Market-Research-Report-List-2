<p><p>Laboratory vacuum pumps are essential equipment in scientific research and industrial applications. Two common types of vacuum pumps are dry vacuum pumps and rotary vane vacuum pumps. Dry vacuum pumps operate without any lubricants, making them suitable for applications where contamination is a concern. Rotary vane vacuum pumps use rotating vanes to create vacuum pressure, making them versatile and reliable for a wide range of laboratory needs. Both types play a crucial role in creating and maintaining a vacuum environment for various experiments and processes.</p></p>
