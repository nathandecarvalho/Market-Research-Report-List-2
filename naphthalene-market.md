<p><p>Naphthalene can be derived from two main sources, coal tar and petroleum. In the coal tar market, naphthalene is produced as a byproduct of the coal carbonization process. This type of naphthalene is typically of lower purity and used in applications such as mothballs and insecticides. On the other hand, naphthalene derived from petroleum is considered higher quality and is used in the production of chemicals, resins, and plastics. Both markets play a crucial role in meeting the demand for naphthalene in various industries.</p></p>
