<p><p>Geotextile fabrics are divided into two main types: synthetic and natural. Synthetic geotextiles are made from materials like polypropylene, polyester, or polyethylene and are known for their strength and durability. They are commonly used in construction projects to provide stabilization and drainage. On the other hand, natural geotextiles are made from materials like jute, coir, or straw and are biodegradable and eco-friendly. They are often used in erosion control and landscaping applications. Both types of geotextiles serve different purposes and cater to specific market needs.</p></p>
